# Use free-tier-first models

Thoughtline will initially route every AI capability to `gemini-3.5-flash` and use `openai/gpt-oss-120b` as the sole Cross-Provider Fallback, avoiding paid-only models and an intermediate same-provider retry. Model IDs, context allowances, completion budgets, and capability support will live in a centralized registry; Groq requests will use a conservative budget below its documented free-plan token ceiling and both adapters will enforce structured output plus local schema validation. Free availability and account-specific limits are provider-controlled and not guaranteed by Thoughtline: onboarding and Connections will link current provider data controls, disclose that Gemini free-tier content may be handled differently from paid-tier content and that Groq retention depends on account controls and limited service exceptions, and never claim zero retention, no training use, or a billing tier that cannot be determined from the key.
