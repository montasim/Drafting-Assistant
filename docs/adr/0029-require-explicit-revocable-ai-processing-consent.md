# Require explicit, revocable AI-processing consent

Thoughtline will require one unchecked, affirmative AI Processing Consent before any Foreground AI Job can transmit content. The onboarding statement that Thoughtline never posts or clicks on LinkedIn is a non-interactive safety note rather than a second consent checkbox, and **Continue** remains disabled until the single required consent is selected. Onboarding and Settings will name Gemini and Groq, explain automatic fallback, enumerate the applicable content categories—including visible LinkedIn text and names, profile data, preferences, pasted drafts, writing samples, user-supplied lessons, and public-source evidence—and state that local workflows contact those providers directly under their current API terms rather than passing through the future scheduling service. The disclosure will link current provider data controls and plainly state that free-tier data use and retention are governed by the user's provider account and may differ from paid or zero-retention configurations. The stored consent record will be versioned so a material scope change requires renewed agreement. Revocation immediately blocks new provider calls while preserving access to local editing, copying, History, settings, credentials, profile data, and learned preferences until the user removes those through their dedicated controls.
