# Use feedback for explicit personalization

Thumb ratings, explicit reply-direction selections, and substantial generated-to-edited changes will be functional Feedback Evidence rather than decorative controls. Like and dislike are mutually exclusive and attach to the exact generated revision being evaluated together with its current edited form; regeneration preserves the prior revision's rating as historical evidence but presents the new revision as unrated. An edit is substantial when normalized word-token edit distance reaches at least the greater of three tokens or ten percent of the generated text's word count; whitespace-only, capitalization-only, and punctuation-only changes are ignored, and the same deterministic Unicode-aware rule applies to English, Bangla, and mixed-language text. The initially displayed reply direction is only a presentation default and becomes evidence only if the user deliberately selects a direction tab. Copying is retained only as a workflow action and does not affect personalization because it does not prove that the user liked or posted the text. Thoughtline will immediately update bounded, deterministic on-device signals for direction ordering, preferred length, paragraph structure, questions, emojis, hashtags, and similar measurable features without starting a provider request. A compact **Learned preferences** disclosure at the bottom of **Tone & voice** shows whether accepted preferences exist and how many feedback examples are available. Its explicit **Review feedback** action is disabled until at least three meaningful examples exist and explains the requirement; an analysis uses at most twelve recent examples, preferring a mix of liked, disliked, and substantially edited drafts across workflows without inferring negative preferences from positive-only evidence. The action starts the provider-neutral Foreground AI Job and opens a focused dialog that previews the exact examples being sent, then presents Zod-validated proposed Learned Writing Preferences for acceptance, editing, or rejection before they affect later prompts. Once a proposal is accepted, its evidence is checkpointed; **Review feedback** becomes available again only after three new meaningful examples, and an incremental analysis receives the accepted summary plus at most twelve new examples without resending previously analyzed raw examples. **Reset learned preferences** requires confirmation, removes the accepted summary, local scores, and analysis checkpoint without deleting raw feedback governed by History, and thereby makes retained examples eligible for a new analysis. Current-job instructions override manual profile and style settings, which override accepted learned preferences, and the product will not represent this mechanism as provider training.
